# Yadira — 3-Minute Demo Reel Script

Tight cut for judges and first-time viewers. One continuous emotional arc:
**meet her world → see it remember → see it protect → see what it costs.**
Same setup as the FAQ video: seeding paste run, two windows for alert shots,
sound ON throughout.

---

**0:00–0:15 — Cold open.**
Black → the logo animation draws itself in (full cycle, with the heartbeat
underline). No narration until the wordmark completes.
> "This is Yadira. The companion who remembers, for the people who forget."

**0:15–0:45 — Her morning.** *(patient window)*
Camp: Hattie + the campfire crackling. Tap "Calm & content" (mm-hm chime).
Leave camp (farewell notes) → chat greeting speaks aloud.
> "Every day starts at camp with Hattie — one tap, no wrong answers, and it
> quietly becomes the caregiver's mood chart. The fire grows warmer every day
> she visits. It never goes out — there is no failure state in her world."

**0:45–1:15 — It remembers.** *(patient window)*
Type "Tell me about your dog, Barnaby" — bubbles land one thought at a time.
Tap "📷 Look at our photos" → photobook → "Tell me about this photo, Yadira"
→ she continues from the photo.
> "Replies arrive one thought at a time, at a human pace. Family photos live
> in a real album Yadira can see — and what she learns today, she still knows
> tomorrow. A disconnection is a pause, not a forgetting."

**1:15–1:45 — Both sides of care.** *(split: caregiver left, patient right)*
Whip through the Hub: mood chart, co-pilot answering a suggested question,
photo album manager. Then patient taps "I need my caregiver — please come"
→ chime + amber banner lands on the left ~1.5s later.
> "Caregivers get the real picture — moods, sleep, hard moments, and a
> co-pilot grounded in her actual records. And when she needs a human, one
> tap reaches one. Yadira never pretends to replace people."

**1:45–2:20 — Vivid Mode, discovered.** *(both windows)*
Patient mentions Beth three times → green invite banner on the caregiver
side → click "Invite Beth to chat" → patient greeting becomes
"Hello, love. It's me, Beth."
> "When she keeps reaching for someone she misses, Yadira notices — and asks
> the family's permission to gently be that presence. Explicit consent,
> caregiver in control, guardrails in every reply. Discovered through her
> need — not a settings toggle."

**2:20–2:45 — The clearest moment.** *(both windows — the peak)*
Patient types: "You're not really Edward. Edward died years ago."
Honest reply lands. Violet banner + chime on the caregiver side:
*"...a rare moment of clarity... go be with them now, in person."*
> "And in a sudden window of clarity, Yadira will never argue her back into
> a comfortable fiction. She honors it — and the family knows in seconds.
> Because these windows are brief, and they matter most."

**2:45–3:00 — Close.**
Landing-page comparison table, hold on the price row → logo end card.
> "Robot companions cost hundreds. Therapy dolls cost thousands. Yadira is
> free for every family — always. Yadira. The companion who remembers."

---

**Cut-down notes:** if you must save seconds, trim 0:45–1:15 to the photo
album only, and drop the co-pilot beat at 1:15 — never cut the campfire,
the invite, or the lucidity moment. Those three are the product.
