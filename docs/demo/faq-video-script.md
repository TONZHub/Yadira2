# Yadira — FAQ Video Script (full-length)

**Format:** each segment is a question a real caregiver would ask, answered
half by narration, half by the app doing the thing on screen. Film at
1280×800+ in a clean browser profile. Run the seeding paste first
(`seed-camera-ready.md`). For every alert shot, have **two browser windows
side by side** — caregiver left, patient right — the ~1.5s propagation reads
beautifully on camera.

**Setup checklist before recording**
- [ ] Seeding paste run + page reloaded (full campfire, 3 photos in album)
- [ ] Firebase sync configured if you want cross-device shots to be real
- [ ] Live model key set if you want personality contrast (simulation replies
      don't vary by personality)
- [ ] Sound on — the fire crackle, Hattie's marimba, and alert chimes are
      half the warmth
- [ ] Browser zoom 100%, notifications off, no extra tabs

---

## 1. "What is Yadira?" — *~60s*

**On screen:** the landing page, slow scroll: hero → About → the
checks-and-Xs comparison table (pause on the price row) → pricing tiers.

**Narration:**
> "Yadira is an AI companion for people living with dementia — and a working
> toolkit for the people who care for them. It's built on validation therapy:
> the memory-care principle that you meet a person in their reality instead
> of arguing them out of it. Families already spend real money trying to ease
> this loneliness — companion robots, robotic pets, five-hundred-dollar
> therapy dolls. Here's how they compare. And here's Yadira's price for
> families: free. Always."

**Cut:** click "Try Yadira free" → the logo animation draws itself in →
fades to the login card. Let it play in full; it's your brand moment.

---

## 2. "What does my mom actually see?" — *~90s*

**On screen:** tap "I'm a Patient." Camp opens: Hattie, the campfire
crackling at day 7, the mood buttons.

**Narration:**
> "Every day starts at camp with Hattie. One question — how are you feeling
> today — big buttons, no wrong answers. That one tap quietly becomes the
> caregiver's mood chart. And this campfire? It grows a little warmer every
> day she visits. It never goes out. There is no failure state anywhere on
> this screen — a missed day just means warm embers, ready for her."

**Action:** tap a mood (Hattie's "mm-hm" chime) → "Leave camp — talk with
Yadira" (farewell notes) → the chat. Type: **"Tell me about your dog,
Barnaby"** — the reply arrives one thought-bubble at a time.

**Narration:**
> "Notice the pacing. A three-sentence reply is a wall of text to someone
> with dementia — so every thought lands as its own bubble, at a human pace."

**Action:** tap the phone icon → Call Mode rings → connects → speak to it.
> "And for someone who never learned to type: it's just a phone call."

---

## 3. "Does it really remember her?" — *~75s*

**On screen:** patient chat. Tap **"📷 Look at our photos"** → the photobook
opens on the lake-house photo. Page through. Tap **"Tell me about this
photo, Yadira."** The album closes and Yadira picks up the conversation from
the photo.

**Narration:**
> "Everything the family shares is kept. Photos uploaded in chat land in a
> real album — one photo per page, big page-turn buttons — and Yadira knows
> what's in it, so 'let's look at old photos' means these photos. Memories
> the family writes down ground every conversation. And session memory
> carries the thread from one visit into the next: a disconnection is a
> pause, not a forgetting."

---

## 4. "What do I get as the caregiver?" — *~90s*

**On screen:** caregiver window. Tour the Hub top to bottom, briskly: mood
check-ins on the chart → Ask-Yadira co-pilot (click a suggested question) →
AI routine → memory bank → photo album manager (recaption a photo on
camera: "names and places help Yadira the most") → FAQ overrides.

**Narration:**
> "The caregiver side turns those conversations into a picture of how she's
> really doing — her moods from camp, her sleep, her hard moments. Ask the
> co-pilot anything; answers come from her actual records, not generic
> advice. Set personalized answers for anxious questions, build routines,
> and curate the album and the memory bank that Yadira speaks from."

**Action:** in the patient window, tap **"I need my caregiver — please
come."** In the caregiver window, the chime + amber banner land ~1.5s later.
> "And when she needs a human, one tap reaches one. Immediately."

---

## 5. "Not everyone wants a soothing therapist." — *~45s*

**On screen:** Hub → Yadira's Personality card. Click through the five.
(Requires live model key for real contrast.)

**Action:** with "Gentle & soothing": ask "What day is it today?" — warm,
soft answer with "dear." Switch to **"Plain & practical"**: same question —
direct, no endearments.

**Narration:**
> "Some people find being cooed at patronizing. So the family chooses who
> Yadira is: gentle, sunny, playful, plain-and-practical, or a storyteller.
> Same safety rails underneath — different person in the room."

---

## 6. "What is Vivid Mode — and is it ethical?" — *~2min. The heart of the video.*

**On screen:** Hub → the signup consent checkboxes (screenshot or re-shoot),
then the mode control card.

**Narration:**
> "When someone reaches for a person from their past — a husband, a
> daughter — correcting them causes pain. Vivid Mode lets Yadira gently be
> that presence. We treat this seriously, because it is serious: it requires
> the caregiver's explicit, informed consent, the caregiver controls it at
> every moment, and guardrails hold in every reply."

**Action:** in the patient window (Lucid mode), mention the persona three
times across three messages — e.g. "Is Beth coming today?", "I miss Beth,"
"Beth always made tea." In the caregiver window the **green invite banner**
appears: *"...has mentioned Beth 3 times today. Would you like to invite
Beth into the conversation?"* Click **Invite**.

**Narration:**
> "And it isn't a settings toggle. Yadira notices who she keeps reaching
> for, and asks the caregiver — would they like to be invited in? The mode
> is discovered through her need, not found in a menu."

**Action:** back on the patient screen — the greeting has become
"Hello, love. It's me, Beth."

---

## 7. "What happens in her clearest moments?" — *~90s. The emotional peak — place last before pricing.*

**On screen:** patient window in Vivid mode. Type:
**"You're not really Edward. Edward died years ago."**

The reply comes back honest: *"You're right, my love. You see everything so
clearly right now. What has always been real is how much you are loved..."*

**Narration (quiet):**
> "In late-stage dementia there are sometimes sudden windows of clarity —
> moments where she knows. In that moment, Yadira will never argue her back
> into a comfortable fiction. She honors it, honestly and warmly."

**On screen:** the caregiver window — chime; the violet banner:
*"Eleanor may be having a rare moment of clarity... If you can, go be with
them now, in person."*

**Narration:**
> "And the family finds out in seconds — not at the next visit. Because
> these windows can be brief, and they can be the most important moments a
> family gets. This is the feature we hope no one ever needs, built the way
> it must be built."

---

## 8. "What if she taps the wrong thing?" — *~45s*

**On screen:** caregiver hands the tablet over (or mime it): tap the **lock
icon** → confirm → the app goes full screen, the tab switcher and logout
vanish. Try tapping the lock — nothing. Hold it — glow — 3 seconds — confirm
→ controls return.

**Narration:**
> "This exists because a grandmother once wiped a family's memories trying
> to zoom in. Care Lock pins the device to her view — everything destructive
> is simply gone from the screen, and the browser bars go with it. Unlocking
> takes a deliberate three-second hold no accidental gesture can perform.
> She can type, talk, look at photos, call for help. She cannot break
> anything."

---

## 9. "What does it cost?" — *~30s, closing*

**On screen:** landing page pricing; end card on the logo animation.

**Narration:**
> "The companion is free for families — no ads, no data selling, no credit
> card. Caregiver Pro is five dollars a week for unlimited AI care reports,
> and facility partnerships keep the lights on. Because a daughter should
> never have to reach for a credit card to hear her mother's voice.
> Yadira — the companion who remembers, for the people who forget."

| Calm redirect | `Help me feel calm, I am a bit anxious.` |
